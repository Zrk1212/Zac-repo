About the QBL

Born in a Chappaqua backyard. Built by seniors. Played for the love of the game.

Our Story

The Quaker Wiffle Ball League was founded in 2026 by a group of seniors at Horace Greeley High School in Chappaqua, New York. What started as a few friends tossing a wiffle ball around in a backyard quickly evolved into something far bigger — a full-fledged competitive league with five teams, six dedicated stadiums, and an ever-growing reputation across Westchester County.

Named for the Quakers — Horace Greeley's legendary mascot — the QBL channels the school's competitive spirit, community pride, and long tradition of excellence. These aren't just pick-up games. Every matchup is a battle for bragging rights, and every season ends with one team lifting the QBL Championship Trophy.

As seniors, we wanted to create something that would outlast our time at Greeley. The QBL is more than a league — it's a legacy. A way to take the bonds forged in high school hallways and carry them out onto the field one last time before we scatter across the country for college.
